Pink Floyd

The Wall

One of the best-selling albums of all time - later adapted into a 1982 film and a theatrical live show, cementing its status as one of rock's most iconic concept albums.

About the Album

Released on November 30, 1979, The Wall is Pink Floyd's eleventh studio album and one of the most ambitious rock operas ever created. The album explores themes of isolation, war, fame, authoritarianism, and personal trauma, following the story of Pink, a rock star who builds a metaphorical wall around himself as he descends into madness.
The album features legendary tracks such as “Another Brick in the Wall, Part 2,” “Comfortably Numb,” “Hey You,” and “Run Like Hell.” Musically, it blends progressive rock with theatrical elements, orchestral arrangements, and emotionally charged lyrics, largely driven by Roger Waters' vision.
Recorded at various studios across the UK, France, and the US, The Wall was produced by Bob Ezrin, David Gilmour, and Roger Waters. Its cover, designed by Gerald Scarfe, features a stark white brick wall, symbolizing the protagonist's emotional and psychological isolation.
