Wish You Were Here

A haunting reflection on absence, the music industry, and the loss of artistic integrity.

About the Album

Released on September 12, 1975, Wish You Were Here is Pink Floyd's ninth studio album, following the massive success of The Dark Side of the Moon. The album delves into themes of absence, disillusionment with the music industry, and the loss of former bandmate Syd Barrett due to mental health struggles.

The album features iconic tracks such as “Shine On You Crazy Diamond,” “Welcome to the Machine,” “Have a Cigar,” and “Wish You Were Here.” Musically, it continues Pink Floyd's progressive rock style, incorporating extended instrumental sections, synthesizers, and evocative lyrics.

Recorded at Abbey Road Studios and produced by Pink Floyd, the album is known for its atmospheric soundscapes, use of studio effects, and emotional depth. The cover, designed by Hipgnosis, features a now-famous image of two businessmen shaking hands—one of them on fire—symbolizing the album's themes of deception and detachment.
